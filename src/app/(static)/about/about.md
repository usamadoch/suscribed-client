1. Hero
Commons is where Pakistani creators turn an audience into income.

2. The Problem

Most creators in Pakistan run their business through WhatsApp groups and bank transfers. Someone pays, someone checks a screenshot, someone adds them to a group by hand. It works—until you have a hundred people to manage.

And on live streams? If your audience is mostly Pakistani, that door is closed. YouTube’s Super Chat isn't available here, leaving money on the table every time you go live.

3. What Commons Does
Commons gives you one page where people can subscribe, chat, and support you live. Payments run on JazzCash, Easypaisa, and local cards, so PKR in, PKR out. No screenshots to check. No groups to manage by hand.

4. Publish and Earn
[Screenshot: post composer / feed — placed right of this text]
Write a post, share a video, or upload a file. Decide who gets to see it: everyone, or only the people paying you. It is the same feed you'd post to anyway, except now it earns.

5. No Shadowbans. Your Platform, Your Rules.
We all know how public platforms police content. But if you think a foreign paywall protects you—believe me, they ban people there, too. One misunderstood conversation, and your entire income stream is gone. On Commons, there are no foreign moderation algorithms dictating what you can say. Your stage, your rules.

6. Built to Convert
[Screenshot: membership/tier page — placed left of this text]
One link for your audience. They see what they get, pick a plan, and pay in rupees. No redirecting them to a foreign checkout page that asks for a currency they don't use.

7. A Private Space for Your Community
[Screenshot: private + group chat — placed right of this text]
Direct messages for the one-on-one conversations. Group chat for the community you're building. Both built in, both included, neither bolted on as an afterthought.

8. Go Live. Get Paid.
[Screenshot: live control room / Super Message in chat — placed left of this text]
Keep streaming on YouTube the way you already do. Commons sits next to it. Your Pakistani audience can send a paid message during the stream, it shows up highlighted in chat, and you read it live. The Super Chat YouTube never gave them.

9. Priced for Pakistan
Supporting you on an international platform means paying for exchange rates, hidden bank fees, and the inflation gap. A small gesture in dollars becomes a heavy burden in rupees. Commons runs natively in PKR, giving your audience the exact same premium experience at a price that actually respects their wallet.

10. Built for Pakistan, Not Translated for It
Every payment runs through JazzCash, Easypaisa, or a local card. Your audience never sees a dollar sign, never wonders about the exchange rate, never gets stopped at checkout because their card isn't supported.

11. Who Commons Is For
Commons is for creators whose audience already wants to support them, but hasn't had a frictionless way to do it:

    Political & Current Affairs: Where audiences loyally follow every episode.
    Religious Scholars & Speakers: Running live Q&As and discussions.
    Teachers & Tutors: Building a paying, dedicated student base.
    Gaming & Entertainment: Creators with a live, highly interactive audience.
